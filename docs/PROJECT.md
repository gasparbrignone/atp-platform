# ATP Platform

## Project Overview

ATP Platform is the official digital platform of **ATP**, a student organization at the **Facultad de Ciencias Médicas** at the **Universidad Nacional de Rosario (UNR), Argentina**.

This platform is designed to centralize academic resources, student activities, institutional information, and digital tools into a single, modern, intuitive, and highly maintainable website.

The project is not intended to be a simple landing page or a Linktree replacement. It is conceived as a scalable digital ecosystem that will evolve over time and become the primary online platform for ATP.

---

# Mission

Provide students with quick, organized and reliable access to everything ATP offers while strengthening the organization's identity and encouraging participation.

The platform should help students:

* Discover upcoming activities.
* Access academic resources.
* Find useful tools for their degree.
* Stay informed.
* Join ATP.
* Feel part of a student community.

---

# Vision

Become the central digital hub for students of the Facultad de Ciencias Médicas.

The website should be useful not only during admission but throughout the entire university journey.

Every new feature should reinforce this objective.

---

# About ATP

ATP is a student organization composed of students working for students.

Its communication style is approachable, clear and practical.

ATP does not communicate as an institution or as a corporation.

Instead, it communicates from the perspective of fellow students who understand university life and seek to improve it.

This identity must remain visible throughout the entire platform.

---

# Design Philosophy

The experience should feel:

* modern
* lightweight
* intuitive
* trustworthy
* elegant
* student-centered

Visual complexity should be low.

Functional value should be high.

Every interface should feel calm and organized.

---

# Target Audience

Primary users:

* Medicine students
* Nursing students
* Speech Therapy students
* Occupational Therapy students

Secondary users:

* Prospective students.
* Former students.
* Collaborating teachers.
* Students from other universities looking for educational resources.

---

# User Experience Principles

Every feature should answer one or more of these questions:

* How does this help a student?
* How does this reduce friction?
* How does this save time?
* How does this encourage participation?

If a feature does not improve student experience, reconsider its inclusion.

---

# Communication Tone

The communication style should be:

Friendly.

Natural.

Student-to-student.

Clear.

Direct.

Optimistic.

Respectful.

Avoid:

* corporate language
* bureaucratic language
* unnecessary academic formalities
* marketing clichés

The website should sound like ATP speaking.

---

# Brand Identity

The platform should reinforce ATP's identity at every opportunity without becoming visually overwhelming.

The ATP logo should appear consistently.

Calls to action inviting students to join ATP should be present across the site where appropriate.

Students should always have an easy way to contact the organization.

---

# Primary Goals

The platform should:

Increase participation in ATP activities.

Improve access to academic material.

Reduce the time students spend searching for information.

Organize educational resources.

Strengthen ATP's visual identity.

Provide long-term value to students.

---

# Secondary Goals

Improve discoverability through search engines.

Support future multilingual content.

Provide useful analytics.

Support future feature expansion.

---

# Site Structure

The website is organized into the following major sections.

## Home

The homepage is the main entry point.

It should immediately communicate:

Who ATP is.

What ATP offers.

What students can do next.

The homepage should include:

* Hero section.
* Upcoming activities.
* News.
* Featured resources.
* Academic tools.
* Degree shortcuts.
* Join ATP call to action.
* Footer.

The homepage should remain visually clean despite containing multiple sections.

---

## Activities

This section promotes ATP events.

Examples:

Courses.

Workshops.

Meetings.

Academic support activities.

Training sessions.

Every activity should include:

Title.

Cover image.

Description.

Date.

Time.

Registration button.

Status.

Expired activities should no longer appear as active.

---

## News

The platform should support visual announcements.

News may include:

Images.

Short text.

Important notices.

Institutional reminders.

Academic information.

The presentation should prioritize quick reading.

---

## Digital Library

The digital library is one of the platform's most important features.

Its objective is to make academic resources easy to discover.

The library should not expose folder structures.

Instead, it should behave like a searchable catalog.

Students should be able to filter by:

Subject.

Career.

Year.

Author.

Resource type.

Edition.

Every resource should provide a direct download or access link.

Current resources are primarily hosted on Google Drive.

---

## Careers

Each degree should have its own landing page.

Initially:

Medicine.

Nursing.

Speech Therapy.

Occupational Therapy.

Each career page should contain:

Useful tools.

Important links.

Academic resources.

Specific information.

Future content.

---

## Medicine

Medicine currently contains the largest amount of content.

Possible resources include:

Academic calendar.

Virtual microscopes.

3D anatomy.

Cadaveric preparations.

External educational tools.

Study resources.

---

## Join ATP

The invitation to participate in ATP should be visible across multiple sections.

Possible contact methods include:

Google Forms.

Instagram.

WhatsApp.

Future communication channels.

The objective is to lower the barrier to participation.

---

# Content Strategy

Content should remain easy to update.

Editors should not modify source code.

Academic resources.

Activities.

Announcements.

Links.

Images.

Should all be manageable through the content management workflow.

---

# Visual Direction

The website should feel inspired by Apple's design philosophy.

Characteristics include:

Minimalism.

Generous spacing.

Balanced typography.

Subtle depth.

Soft animations.

Visual hierarchy.

Calm interfaces.

Never sacrifice usability for visual effects.

---

# Performance Philosophy

Students frequently access the platform using mobile networks.

The website should therefore prioritize:

Fast loading.

Small bundles.

Optimized images.

Minimal JavaScript.

Smooth scrolling.

Responsive interactions.

---

# Accessibility

Accessibility is a core requirement.

The platform should be usable regardless of:

Input method.

Device.

Screen size.

Assistive technologies.

Accessibility should never be treated as an optional enhancement.

---

# Scalability

The architecture should allow future features without requiring redesign.

Potential future additions include:

Interactive academic planners.

Exam schedule management.

Volunteer management.

Member area.

Student dashboards.

Integrated registrations.

Push notifications.

Multilingual support.

---

# Success Criteria

The project is successful if students can:

Find information quickly.

Register for activities easily.

Download resources effortlessly.

Navigate intuitively.

Recognize ATP's identity immediately.

Return regularly because the platform provides ongoing value.

---

# Non-Goals

The platform is not intended to become:

A social network.

A university management system.

An LMS.

A replacement for official university platforms.

Instead, it complements existing institutional resources while providing a better student experience.

---

# Long-Term Vision

This project should become the digital reference point for ATP.

Every future decision should reinforce four principles:

1. Help students.
2. Strengthen community.
3. Simplify access to information.
4. Build a platform that remains useful for many years.
